How do I sell a dress on Reloved?

Click on the 'Sell' link in pop out Navigation Menu to get started.

I've created a post, what happens now?

    Immediately after you create your post:
  • Your post appears instantly on the app and you can see it in View All and Just In (accessible from Home page).
  • If you want to remove it at any time, go to My Listed Items on Home page to do this.

    • What happens when my dress is sold?

  • Buyer pays Reloved directly, through the app.
  • After we've processed the payment (which is instant) we send you an email notifying you of the sale.
  • Within 2 days of receiving our email, you need to pack and ship your dress to us. Address and mailing instructions will be in our email
  • We receive your dress, check it and then re-pack it and ship onwards to the buyer. The buyer has 7 days to return the dress. If they do return it, the dress comes back to us and we send it onwards to you.
  • Assuming no returns, within 14 days of the sale, we will mail you a cheque for the sale proceeds.

What are best practices for selling through Reloved?

  • Create high quality pictures
  • Provide detailed descriptions
  • Set price competitively

When you take pictures, practices that help are:

  • Use white background;
  • Model the dress yourself;
  • Take multiple pictures, the more the better;
  • Ensure bright lighting;
  • 'Cut your head off' when you edit the pictures.

How do I find out when my dress is sold?

You will receive an email from us.

Do I have to offer returns?

Yes. All dresses sold on Reloved carry a 7 day return option. This will actually help you more than you think.

How will I get paid?

We'll send a cheque to the address listed in your contact details (access via Home page in the App).

When will I get paid?

We'll mail a cheque to you within 14 days of the sale being completed.

Can the buyer see my contact details?

At no point will the buyer see your contact details or be able to contact you directly; as a seller you always deal only with the Reloved team.
